2.1 Research Paper · Co-authored

Hierarchical Coarse-to-Fine Framework for Concrete Crack Detection

With Dr. Wisam Bukaita · Lawrence Technological University

A reliability-aware three-stage deep learning framework for automated concrete structural inspection. A MobileNetV2 classifier screens 56K+ images before forwarding only crack-positive candidates to a SegFormer-B2 segmentation model at 384×384 resolution with test-time augmentation. A geometry stage estimates crack length and width from predicted masks. Final segmentation model selected after benchmarking 10+ architectures including U-Net variants, Attention U-Net, DeepLabV3+, U-Net++ ResNet34, EfficientNet-B4 U-Net++, and SegFormer-B0/B2. Includes domain-shift evaluation on DeepCrack and pipeline-level reliability metrics.

Stage 1 accuracy91.68% · ROC-AUC 0.934 · Macro-F1 0.844
Stage 2 Dice0.7572 · IoU 0.6353 · pixel recall 0.7817
Pipeline efficiency85.06% of images filtered before segmentation
Domain adaptationDeepCrack Dice → 0.6553 after two-stage adaptation
StackPyTorch · SegFormer · MobileNetV2 · U-Net++ · Transfer Learning · Domain Adaptation

2.2 Research Project · Interpretable AI

Neuro-Symbolic Hybrid Classification System

Collaborative Research Project · Lawrence Technological University

A two-tier hybrid AI architecture combining Probabilistic Inductive Logic Programming (P-ILP) rules with a calibrated Multinomial Naive Bayes classifier to address the accuracy–interpretability trade-off in text categorization. Classifies the HuffPost News Category Dataset (209,527 articles across 10 categories) with 100% coverage. Four fusion strategies were implemented and compared; Confidence Routing was selected for optimal accuracy–interpretability balance. Formal Expected Calibration Error analysis confirms confidence scores genuinely reflect prediction reliability.

Overall accuracy82.85% across 209,527 articles
Interpretable coverage24.3% rule-based · 75.7% calibrated ML · 100% total
Calibration qualityECE 1.4% (target < 5%) · Rule-ML fidelity 91.3%
ApproachAnte-hoc interpretability · PERML architecture pattern
StackPython · scikit-learn · TF-IDF · NetworkX · P-ILP

2.3 Applied NLP · Exploratory Analysis

Social Network Sentiment Analysis for Mental Health Awareness

With Bhavyasri Lakkamraju · Lawrence Technological University

An end-to-end NLP and exploratory data analysis pipeline examining 38,000+ Reddit posts from mental-health-focused subreddits. Combines pre-trained sentiment models (VADER for three-class rule-based labeling, DistilBERT for transformer-based binary classification) with K-means thematic clustering on TF-IDF features and PCA-based visualization. Designed to surface trends, themes, and tonal patterns useful for mental health awareness campaigns.

Dataset38,033 Reddit posts · multiple mental-health subreddits
Sentiment modelsVADER (3-class) + DistilBERT (binary)
Thematic clusteringK-means (k=5) on TF-IDF · PCA visualization
